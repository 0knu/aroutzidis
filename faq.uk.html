Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about my services

What is psychotherapy?

Psychotherapy (therapy of the soul) constitutes the basic structural component for all forms of psychological processes that aim to address psychosomatic illnesses, mental trauma or behavioral dysfunctions, without pharmaceutical treatment.

From the perspective of the science of psychology or psychotherapy research, psychotherapy constitutes a special kind of controlled human relationship, with a scientific background, in which the therapist creates the special preconditions and conditions, so as to make possible, for one or more patients, changes that aim to treat or limit mental/physical problems.

For this to happen, systematically scientifically documented methods of verbal and non-verbal communication are used. There are various forms/schools with recognized methods and they are partially specialized in specific mental problems. This fact often makes it difficult for the interested party to choose the appropriate therapy, without knowing which method is most suitable for their case.

Personal development can also be associated with psychotherapy or even constitute an exclusive goal. The quality and formation of the relationship as well as the mobilization of the patient by the psychotherapist, increase the patient's ability to manage their problems and acquire increasing mental and physical well-being.

I have noticed that there is a large offer in biofeedback therapies, with impressive equipment. What are the criteria for selecting the appropriate specialist, so that I can receive this specific therapy?

Biofeedback therapy should only be applied by properly trained specialists. Abroad, the title and application of biofeedback therapy is protected and applied only by people trained by an official biofeedback company. Only these people are entitled to bear the title of biofeedback therapist. In short, the title of biofeedback therapist as well as the practice of biofeedback therapy is held and applied only by doctors, psychologists trained in psychotherapy and/or clinical psychology, following a training protocol from the corresponding official biofeedback company. The training, depending on the company and the difficulty index of obtaining the biofeedback therapist certification, varies. The Austrian and German biofeedback and psychophysiology companies have a training protocol of 150 hours with 10 hours of supervised application of therapy, as well as mandatory case presentation work.

People who are not doctors or psychologists with training in clinical psychology or psychotherapy do not hold the title nor practice biofeedback therapy. However, they can be trained as biofeedback coaches with fewer training hours but definitely 10 hours of supervision and final work.

The training protocol provided by such companies ensures expertise and suitability in applying therapy to patients. In Greece, unfortunately, as a specific sector it is not protected and not registered. However, the interested party seeking biofeedback therapy can and should ask the "so-called" therapist to be informed about the level of training they have in this specific field. Then they will choose according to their judgment.

Regarding the issue of biofeedback devices, certainly a complex device can show more psychophysiological variables and/or impress. However, the best tool is useless in the hands of an improperly and inadequately trained person. Therapy protocols are taught by appropriate certified and official bodies. The device does not make the therapist!!!

I have tension-type headaches. How can biofeedback therapy help me? Can biofeedback therapy offer me a permanent solution to the problem? If yes, after how many sessions?

Tension-type headaches are usually created by continuous muscle contraction in the area of the trapezius, neck and nape muscles, as well as in the muscle groups of the face. Also, psychosomatic, mental factors and stress can be causes of their creation. Another possible cause is the incorrect posture of the neck and spine.

With biofeedback therapy, the person can learn consciously to control the muscle tension of the muscles related to the pain sensation and through repetition this is assimilated as a skill. Also, the person learns the psychophysiological parameters that appear during tension-type headache, resulting in their reduction most of the time. In the case where incorrect body posture is responsible, the person is trained using always biofeedback multi-device to keep their body correctly, appropriately coordinating muscle tension, something that the trained biofeedback therapist will indicate.

Over time and with the development of the above skill, the cause of muscle tension has been regulated and consequently the consequence of tension, which is pain, has been eliminated. The time duration varies according to protocol between 10 and 20 sessions, although this is not always absolute.

(As always applies in dysfunctions with physical symptoms, appropriate examination and evaluation by the doctor of the corresponding specialty is necessary).

Are clinical psychotherapy/sleep therapy and hypnosis synonymous concepts?

Clinical psychotherapy / sleep therapy constitutes one of the most prevalent forms of psychotherapy worldwide. Its basic characteristic compared to other forms of psychotherapy is that the psychotherapeutic process takes place in the patient's subconscious. It is therefore the therapy that takes place "in sleep", in a different state of consciousness. The term hypnosis, as originally formulated, because there was previously the view that the patient is in deep sleep, is now considered outdated and anachronistic. However, it prevailed and is used to this day. We could periphrastically describe hypnosis as focused attention within oneself and one's internal processes, i.e. attention turned to the subconscious. In contrast to the conscious "mind" which can be described as "procedural mind", i.e. the part of the self that deals with practical matters and logical/rational processes. The subconscious, on the other hand, functions in a holistic form, symbolically, but particularly emotionally.

By emotion we mean the biological substance of emotions, the seat of which, as is known, is the amygdala of the brain. Psychotherapy in sleep aims to reshape unpleasant emotions and free the patient from the continuous incomplete cycle of experiencing these emotions. This psychotherapeutic process is called in sleep therapy terminology subconscious therapy. Its cornerstone is the so-called emotional reshaping, i.e. the release of unpleasant emotions and the deeper realization that they belong to the past. Through this, the elimination of the consequences that repressed subconscious emotions have will occur. Any form of sleep therapy must achieve emotional reshaping in order to be able to claim that it has actually achieved some therapeutic result.

Hypnosis by itself is simply a form of state of the person's consciousness, as described above. Given that there is no hypnosis but only self-hypnosis, essentially we are talking about a natural state in which anyone can find themselves. When we talk about hypnosis we are not referring to some form of psychotherapy, but only to the state. The known suggestions in hypnosis, however impressive they may be, do not in any case constitute a form of therapy. On the contrary, due to the hypnotic state, the person, more because the factor of rationalism has been bypassed, accepts for some time the suggested messages subconsciously. Something similar happens with advertisements where there are suggestions, which are not made conscious by the conscious mind but only by the subconscious. The purpose of every form of psychotherapy is the identification of the traumatic experience and its reshaping or reconstruction.

If this structural component is missing then it is not therapy. Hypnotic suggestions find fertile ground for some time, but most of the time the symptoms that are eliminated return more severely. For this reason, in a responsible form of therapy, suggestions are combined with subconscious therapy, and if the latter is successfully completed, suggestions are usually not necessary.

So there should be no confusion of the term hypnosis with the term sleep therapy or clinical psychotherapy/sleep therapy, which is the full term.

In the first case we are not talking about a form of psychotherapy.

I have panic attacks with intense symptoms. Can biofeedback help me get rid of its symptoms?

Panic attacks are characterized by intense symptoms that the person experiences and are extremely annoying. This fearful reaction manifests intensely in the body and causes a series of psychophysiological changes during the occurrence of the panic attack.

Biofeedback therapy can certainly help the person to control and reduce or even eliminate these symptoms, through conscious observation and conscious control of psychophysiological parameters responsible for the appearance of symptoms. Through techniques (specific ones) that the patient learns from the biofeedback therapist, they ultimately achieve the above control. The reproduction of the patient's psychophysiological variables in a visual/auditory way helps them understand their nature as well as control their course at all times.

It is important, of course, to diagnose if there is also agoraphobia, which usually occurs, which must also be addressed. Then the therapeutic process must be completed psychotherapeutically. However, biofeedback therapy is extremely suitable and effective, provided that in the panic attack specific psychophysiological differentiation has been identified and there is also the corresponding specific therapeutic technique to relieve the person from the characteristic tendency to faint and the dizziness they have, as well as from the high pulse rate.

As always applies in dysfunctions with physical symptoms, appropriate examination and evaluation by the doctor of the corresponding specialty is necessary.

Can a person with concentration problems or attention deficit improve their condition through biofeedback? The prevailing opinion is that through the electroencephalograph of neurofeedback the best possible results can be achieved, which form of biofeedback is ultimately the most appropriate?

Every form of biofeedback is useful and can be utilized by the specialist. In attention deficit or even in attention concentration problems, the purpose of biofeedback is to increase the brain activity of points in the brain that are involved in mental activity. In this way, mental performance will also increase. For example, an athlete who wants to increase their performance in speed will train their legs so that they can increase both strength and speed simultaneously.

There are physiological variables that can be mentally influenced, at will, and increase the ability to focus attention, which also implies better mental performance. The most well-known form of biofeedback, until recent years, was the neurofeedback of the electroencephalograph, also known as EEG. This deals with brain activity through brain electrical waves. However, its application in practice has great difficulty because the precise placement of the electrodes takes a lot of time and their number is large. Also, the measurements we get from the electroencephalograph are very sensitive to movements that the patient may make, which cannot be avoided, resulting in the specialist having to filter the real measurements and those coming from movements in the graph. The EEG also measures the surface activity of the cerebral cortex (postsynaptic potentials) and the control of these activities is more subconscious than conscious. It certainly is a very good tool but has practical problems in application and complicates both the therapist and the patient considerably.

The new form of neurofeedback HEG, the so-called hemoencephalograph, is gaining more and more supporters as it constitutes the solution to the problems that the electroencephalograph had. It is very easy to apply since the sensor is placed easily in the forehead area. According to leading neuroanatomist Walle, the frontal cortex constitutes the most important meeting point of information from the sensory area and the limbic nervous system. It is the point that is related to personal emotions, motivations and subjective knowledge. Due to its strategic anatomical position, the frontal cortex is the largest system of human emotional behavior.

In the HEG hemoencephalograph, brain activity is measured through the differentiation of oxygen supply. Cognitive processes stimulate neurons in specific points of the brain. These consume energy which leads to a local increase in blood supply. Neurofeedback with the hemoencephalograph makes it possible to increase blood supply to selected points of the brain.

This specialized characteristic of HEG makes it the most suitable tool for increasing the ability to focus attention, and consequently for addressing attention deficit. The person can consciously during the session activate the relevant points of the brain and greatly improve their attention or concentration problem. The research documenting the superiority of the hemoencephalograph in neurofeedback is increasing more and more.

However, beyond neurofeedback, one can be helped with concentration and attention problems through electrodermal biofeedback and appropriate psychoeducational software. Of course, the specialist must have expertise and experience in evaluating the graph of electrodermal activity and must always be able to explain the course of the graph curve. In this case, this type of biofeedback is also excellent and can help.

After all, there is no therapeutic intervention that is so accurate and effective in the problem of attention deficit and the increase of mental performance.
